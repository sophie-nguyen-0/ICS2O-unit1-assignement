Ada Lovelace

1815 – 1852

English mathematician and writer

“The more I study, the more insatiable do I feel my genius for it to be.”

Ada Lovelace was one of the influential mathematicians and English writers of her time. She has written instructions for the first computer program in the mid 1800s. She also wrote the program for Charles Babbage’s mechanical computer, the analytical engine. She was born on December 10, 1815 to the poet Lord Byron and Anne Isabella Milbanke.

She was also the only legitimate child of her father but had no relationship with her father. Lord Byron left his wife and daughter continuing his affair with a stage actress and died when Ada was just nine years old.
